Why LeasePlan / Ayvens Damage Charges Are So Common

LeasePlan and Ayvens collect vehicles in high volume. Their inspections are fast, automated and often inconsistent. As a result, many customers receive invoices that:

  • Do not match the condition of the vehicle when it left you
  • Include pre-existing wear and tear
  • Add inflated repair prices
  • Charge for items that should be classed as fair wear

What Counts as Fair Wear?

Under the BVRLA (British Vehicle Rental & Leasing Association) guidance, the following usually fall under fair wear:

  • Light scratches smaller than 25mm
  • Small stone chips
  • Interior marks from normal use
  • Tyre wear within legal limits
  • Minor alloy scuffing

If the charge relates to fair wear, it is not enforceable.

Your Rights When Disputing a Leasing Charge

You can legally demand:

  • Full photographic evidence
  • The inspector’s report
  • The pre-collection condition report
  • Breakdowns of repair costs
  • The repair invoice from the garage

Most successful disputes are won because the evidence does not support the invoice.

How to Challenge LeasePlan / Ayvens Charges

1. Request the Evidence Pack

Ask for all photos, videos, notes, and the inspector’s log. If they refuse — that helps your case.

2. Compare the Evidence with BVRLA Guidelines

If the damage falls under fair wear, highlight this immediately.

3. Challenge the Repair Costs

Many charges include repair prices that are far higher than market rate. Ask them to justify the costs.

4. Dispute Any Inconsistencies

If the vehicle passed a pre-collection walk-around, this is one of the strongest points you can use.

5. Send a Structured Legal Complaint

This is where most people need help — LeasePlan / Ayvens will not back down unless the complaint is written correctly and applies the right legislation.

When You Should Not Pay

Do not pay if any of the following apply:

  • The evidence is unclear or incomplete
  • The damage was already on the vehicle
  • The price seems inflated
  • The damage is within fair wear guidelines
  • The report contradicts the pre-collection inspection
  • The charge appeared weeks after collection
