How to Fight Unfair Charges in the UK (2025 Guide)

If you’ve received an unfair fee, damage charge, admin fee or penalty — you can dispute it. This guide explains your rights and the exact steps to overturn unfair charges.

Why Unfair Charges Happen

Unfair charges are becoming increasingly common in the UK. Companies rely on consumers not knowing their rights — and many people simply pay because they feel intimidated or confused.

Most unfair charges fall into one of these categories:

  • Incorrect or inflated fees
  • Charges with no evidence
  • Damage fees that were pre-existing
  • Admin fees that are unlawful or excessive
  • Penalties for things outside your control
  • Estate agents or landlords inventing costs
  • LeasePlan/Ayvens ‘wear and tear’ charges
  • Businesses refusing to refund you

Your Legal Rights

Under UK consumer law, a company cannot charge you a fee that is:

  • Unreasonable
  • Not explained upfront
  • Not supported by evidence
  • Disproportionate to the issue
  • Used as a penalty (penalties are illegal)

If a charge fails any of these tests, you are legally entitled to challenge it.

How to Fight an Unfair Charge (Step-by-Step)

1. Request Evidence

You are legally entitled to see:

  • Invoices
  • Photos
  • Reports
  • Breakdowns of costs
  • Call logs or inspection notes

If the company cannot provide evidence, the charge is unenforceable.

2. Ask for a Justification

Simply say: “Please explain how this charge is reasonable and lawful.”

Most companies cannot justify their own fees.

3. Point Out Consumer Law

Under the Consumer Rights Act 2015, terms must be:

  • Transparent
  • Fair
  • Proportionate

Anything that resembles a penalty is unlawful.

4. Send a Formal Complaint

This is the step where most people give up — because writing the email is overwhelming.

5. Escalate to External Bodies (If Required)

Depending on the company, you may be able to escalate to:

  • Financial Ombudsman Service
  • Motor Ombudsman
  • Property Ombudsman
  • ICO (for data issues)
  • County Court (for recovery)

When You Should NOT Pay a Charge

You should dispute the charge if any of the following are true:

  • You were not warned about it
  • The fee is excessive
  • It feels like a penalty
  • The evidence doesn’t match the claim
  • You feel pressured, threatened, or manipulated
  • Other customers have complained about the same issue
  • The company is refusing to explain the fee properly
