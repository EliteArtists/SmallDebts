Estate Agent Won’t Respond? Here’s What To Do (2025 Guide)

Whether you're a tenant chasing a repair, a buyer needing updates, or a seller waiting on progress — estate agents who ignore you are more than frustrating. Here’s how to force a response legally and effectively.

Why Estate Agents Ignore People

Estate and letting agents deal with large workloads and often prioritise the cases that put financial pressure on them. When they ignore you, it usually falls into one of these categories:

  • They believe you're not “urgent”
  • Your request costs them time or money
  • They want to delay dealing with a problem
  • Your complaint could result in compensation
  • A staff member simply hasn’t passed the message on

While frustrating, the law is on your side — especially if repairs, deposits, or contract obligations are involved.

Your Rights (Tenants, Buyers & Sellers)

Under UK law, agents must:

  • Respond within a reasonable timeframe
  • Pass your message to the landlord/seller
  • Act fairly and communicate updates
  • Not ignore repair or safety issues
  • Follow the terms of their management contract

If they don’t, you can escalate.

How To Get an Estate Agent to Respond

1. Send a Written Request (Time-Stamped)

Email is best. WhatsApp is acceptable. Avoid phone-only conversations — they leave no evidence.

Your message should politely request an update and set a clear timeframe for response.

2. Send a Follow-Up Citing Their Legal Obligations

This is where most people struggle. A structured, legally-backed message forces a reply.

Small Debts can send this on your behalf for £12.50.

3. Contact Their Manager or Branch Director

If the negotiator or property manager is ignoring you, escalate internally.

4. Tell Them You Will Escalate to TPO or TDS

Agents dread formal complaints. Mentioning the relevant body often triggers immediate action.

5. Submit a Formal Complaint (If Needed)

You can escalate to:

  • The Property Ombudsman (TPO)
  • Property Redress Scheme (PRS)
  • Tenancy Deposit Scheme (TDS)

Agents are required by law to cooperate with these bodies.

When You Should Take Action Quickly

You should escalate immediately if:

  • Repairs are being ignored
  • Your deposit hasn’t been returned
  • You’re being charged unfair fees
  • Your messages are being blocked or dismissed
  • Your purchase/sale is being jeopardised
