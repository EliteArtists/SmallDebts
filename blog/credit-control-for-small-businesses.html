Credit Control for Small Businesses (2025 Guide)

Late payments are one of the biggest threats to small businesses and freelancers in the UK. If you're constantly chasing invoices or struggling with cash flow, proper credit control can change everything.

Explore Monthly Credit Control Plans

Why Credit Control Matters

Cash flow is the lifeblood of any small business. Even profitable companies can fail if customers take too long to pay. In 2024, UK businesses waited an average of 54 days to be paid — far beyond standard payment terms.

Effective credit control ensures:

  • Invoices are sent on time
  • Chasing is consistent and professional
  • Your tone remains firm but respectful
  • Clients understand your payment expectations
  • Late payments become the exception, not the norm

Why Small Businesses Struggle With Overdue Invoices

Most small teams simply don’t have the time, energy or systems to chase payments consistently. Common problems include:

  • Not wanting to “sound aggressive”
  • Lack of a proper follow-up schedule
  • Letting overdue invoices build up
  • Forgetting to remind clients
  • Not knowing how to escalate

Good credit control removes all of this stress.

How Credit Control Works

A proper credit control system typically includes:

  • Sending invoices promptly
  • Reminder emails at clear intervals (7 days, due date, overdue)
  • Firm, legally informed language where required
  • Late-payment interest and fees (allowed by UK law)
  • Escalation for problem clients

Most debt issues never reach court — they resolve when communication is structured and consistent.

Signs You Need a Credit Control Service

You should consider outsourcing if:

  • You have more than 2 overdue invoices each month
  • You are too busy to chase payments
  • Your clients ignore reminders
  • You hate chasing money yourself
  • Cash flow is unpredictable
  • Admin work is eating into your day
